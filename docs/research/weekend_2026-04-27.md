3. **Custom Silicon and AI Accelerator Diversification Accelerates**
   The Meta–Broadcom AI chip alliance is reportedly targeting TSMC 2nm with CoWoS-L packaging for 1H27, joining Google's TPU roadmap and an emerging NVIDIA LPU project. Marvell is positioned as a multi-vector beneficiary across UALink, CXL, the Google LPU, and memory architecture. The picture: hyperscaler ASIC volume is becoming a structural — not optional — slice of AI compute. *(Sources: TrendForce, Global Semi Research, Diligence Stack)*

4. **Memory and Optical Interconnect Called Out as Structurally Under-Supplied**
   Global Semi Research argues memory capex is "too little, not too much," framing the spend cycle as AI-structural rather than cyclical, and notes LTAs favor suppliers in tight markets. AXT is highlighted as a leveraged play on indium phosphide substrates for AI optical interconnects, with the segment cited at ~85% growth. The Diligence Stack reinforces that memory/storage layer collapse and 800 VDC power re-architecture are the next constraints as rack densities push 120–300+ kW. *(Sources: Global Semi Research, Diligence Stack)*

5. **Intel's Narrative Inflects from Laggard to Leverage**
   Global Semi Research flags CPU scarcity, agentic AI demand, and multi-year hyperscaler contracts as rewriting Intel's investment case — a shift that lines up directly with the TeraFab disclosure timeline. Combined with Wei's acknowledgment of Intel as a credible foundry competitor, the narrative shift is the cleanest sentiment delta of the week. *(Sources: Global Semi Research, TrendForce)*
