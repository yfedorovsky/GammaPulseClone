### **1\. Call-flow conviction features: What actually predicts vs. noise**

Core academic foundation remains Pan & Poteshman (2006): stocks with low put-call ratios constructed from **buyer-initiated open-buy volume** outperform high-ratio names by \>40 bp next day and \>1% over the next week on a risk-adjusted basis. Predictability is stronger in smaller stocks and higher PIN (probability of informed trading) environments and decays gradually as information incorporates. This is **not** all volume—closing trades and non-aggressive prints carry far less edge. Extensions show OTM put-call ratios also predict (sometimes more persistently), and open interest itself contains forward price information beyond volume.⁠Papers.ssrn \+2

In your feature list:

* **Signed aggressor side \+ sweep/ISO condition**: Highest evidentiary support. Aggressive buyer-initiated prints (ASK-side for calls, sweeps hitting multiple venues or ISOs) best proxy informed or committed flow. Passive or bid-side prints more likely facilitation/hedging/market-making.  
* **Multi-strike clustering and multi-tenor laddering**: Logical and practitioner-favored proxies for conviction (large player or informed entity building a view across strikes/expirations). Less directly tested in older papers but aligns with "unusual activity" filters in modern tools; isolated single-strike prints frequently noise or hedging.  
* **Large $ notional and V/OI ratio spikes**: Size filters help (larger prints more likely informed); V/OI (your volume-adjusted OI) highlights building vs. existing positioning. Open interest dynamics carry information content; volume spikes without OI build often ephemeral.[⁠Researchgate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/305194232_Trading_on_the_information_content_of_open_interest_Evidence_from_the_US_equity_options_market)  
* **Moneyness/Δ and DTE**: Context-dependent. OTM or specific Δ often more informative for directional bets or events; shorter DTE for catalysts, longer for structural. High IV percentile often flags hedging or event-chasing rather than pure conviction.  
* **OI change**: Positive when building (especially if accompanied by aggressor prints); less informative on pure volume without side.

**ASK-side call buying** is genuinely more informative on average than passive flow—it reflects aggressive directional demand—but a material fraction remains dealer facilitation or retail leverage chasing rather than pure alpha. Tax/structural flow exists (year-end, etc.) but is secondary. Weighting recommendation for a conviction score: multiplicative or additive points system prioritizing (aggressor/sweep flag × size/notional tier × clustering count × regime alignment) with lighter bonuses for V/OI spike or favorable moneyness/DTE. Down-weight or penalize isolated prints, high-IV event chases without other confirmation, or flow against strong gamma walls. Your low SOE A precision (14.9% win rate) is unsurprising once these filters are applied rigorously; many public "whale" alerts fail similar hurdles.

### **4\. Dealer positioning: GEX, VEX, CEX, OI variants, and conventions**
