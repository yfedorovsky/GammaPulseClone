# In-Depth Analysis of AI Infrastructure Bottlenecks

Jukan consistently frames the AI buildout as a **cascade of interconnected bottlenecks** where hyperscaler/CSP demand and new accelerator platform ramps outpace the supply chain's ability to deliver capacity, yield, and next-generation technology at scale. Below is a structured deep dive by theme, with examples, quantified data points, nuances, and implications drawn directly from his recent output and Substack context.

## 1. Memory (HBM / DRAM / NAND) — Structural Tightness & Pricing Power

**Core Dynamic**: AI (training clusters, inference at scale, in-house accelerators) is driving sustained high demand for high-bandwidth memory and broader DRAM. Supply response (capacity, HBM stack technology) is lagging or shifting, supporting elevated pricing and profits for leaders.

**Key Data Points from Recent Coverage**:
- Goldman Sachs significantly raised Samsung and SK Hynix operating-profit forecasts for 2026–2028 (particularly large lifts in 2027–2028).
- Reports of hyperscaler prepayments (Microsoft reportedly ~$10B to Samsung) and long-term contracts as hard demand signals.
- HBM technology roadmap note: Rubin Ultra 16-high HBM4E now more likely 2028; 8-high/12-high variants positioned for 2027.
- Sell-side framing higher memory prices as an **inflationary headwind** for the broader economy.
- Rising discussion of potential U.S. government intervention risk if "excessive profits" in memory become political consensus.

**Substack Context (Deeper History)**:
- Analysis of the historical "memory chicken game" and which players survived.
- Argument that traditional cyclical boom-bust models are less applicable because AI demand is **structural**.
- Coverage of OpenAI-scale DRAM orders as a fear signal of tightness.
- Bullish NAND case in certain contexts.

**Nuances & Edge Cases**:
- Chinese memory suppliers (e.g., CXMT) are being evaluated by HP/Dell but adoption appears slow/cautious.
- High profits are a double-edged sword: near-term earnings tailwind but potential regulatory/policy backlash.
- Capacity ramps by all players could eventually reintroduce oversupply risk if demand growth moderates or everyone executes well.
- Prepayments/LTAs give visibility and priority allocation to large CSPs; smaller players or spot buyers face more risk.

**Implications**:
- Strong earnings and potential analyst upgrade cycles for Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix.
- Multi-quarter visibility on pricing power in HBM/DRAM.
- Watch for policy or geopolitical developments around memory as a strategic sector.
- Complements momentum trading with fundamental catalyst timing around earnings and analyst revisions.

## 2. Advanced Packaging, Substrates (Glass Core, ABF) & Panel-Level Packaging (PLP)

**Core Dynamic**: Larger AI dies, higher HBM stacks, and power/thermal demands are pushing the limits of traditional organic substrates and wafer-level packaging. Next-generation solutions (glass core substrates, PLP) are being industrialized to solve warpage, power integrity (PI), thermal, and productivity constraints.

**Key Data Points from Recent Coverage**:
- TSMC glass core substrate program (partnered with Ibiden for substrates and Innolux for glass/panel aspects). Validated on large AI GPU-class packages (85×110mm, 5x reticle CoW).
  - Quantified benefits: 16% improvement in COP (warpage), 19% lower effective CTE, 31% higher effective modulus, 27% lower resistance, 42% lower inductance → meaningfully better power integrity.
  - Enables more transistors, higher clock speeds, or power headroom for AI compute.
  - Mass-production target framed as 4Q28–1Q29 to match Nvidia AI chip iteration cadence.
- PLP (panel-level packaging) being accelerated by TSMC for productivity gains (rectangular panels vs. round wafers → significantly more chips per equivalent area). Positioned as direct competition with Samsung in high-volume AI chip packaging.
- ABF (Ajinomoto) remains the dominant interlayer insulating material. CEO commentary: demand is not a passing boom (extends to robotics/physical AI); company planning capacity through 2030+ but cautious on near-term opportunistic price hikes to preserve long-term relationships. New plant in planning (operations targeted 2032).
- Glass expected to **coexist** with ABF rather than fully replace it. Intel and Samsung Electro-Mechanics also advancing glass-substrate roadmaps.

**Nuances & Edge Cases**:
- Biggest technical hurdles for glass: Through-Glass Via (TGV) formation, copper fill quality, long-term thermal reliability, and scaling to large formats without micro-cracks or delamination.
- Substrate cost is still low single-digit % of AI chip BOM, but packaging yield losses are much higher — so even higher-cost glass can be net positive if it improves yield/reliability/performance.
- Execution risk on timelines (glass mass prod still years away; TGV know-how concentrated).
- Competitive intensity rising between TSMC, Samsung, and Intel in next-gen packaging/foundry.

**Implications**:
- Multi-year tailwind for substrate and advanced materials leaders (TSMC, Ibiden, Ajinomoto, Innolux, Samsung Electro-Mechanics).
- Enables continued scaling of AI chip performance and power efficiency.
- Watch for validation milestones, yield data, and customer (Nvidia, AMD, CSPs) adoption signals.
- Strong overlap with user's photonics/power/thermal interests (better PI and thermal matching directly addresses power delivery and cooling bottlenecks at package level).

## 3. Photonics & Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) — InP Substrates & Optical Interconnect

**Core Dynamic**: AI rack densities and bandwidth requirements are pushing optical communications (CPO) as a necessary evolution beyond copper. Indium Phosphide (InP) substrates are a key bottleneck material for high-speed optics.

**Key Data Points from Recent Coverage**:
- China imposed export controls on InP substrates (retaliatory context around Feb 2025). This created a recognized bottleneck for optical communications capacity.
- CSPs (Google and others) prioritizing **supply security** over pure cost. Personal visits to non-China suppliers to signal real, substantial demand. Long-term target discussed: >50% non-China sourcing.
- Some easing of Chinese controls noted (batches released), supporting 2H operations for Taiwan compound semi players (VPEC, GCS), but strategic diversification push continues.
- CPO deployment timeline signals: Small-volume scale-out late 3Q26; broader scale-up in 2H27 (reference to NVL576-class systems).
- UBS note on Largan Precision highlighted for CPO catalysts alongside resilient core smartphone lens demand.

**Nuances & Edge Cases**:
- InP market historically dominated by a small number of suppliers (AXT and Sumitomo ~80% combined). Alternative suppliers (Freiberg Compound Materials, Japanese players, domestic Chinese capacity) are ramping but face yield and scale challenges.
- Risk of future low-price Chinese supply flooding markets if controls fully lift, potentially discouraging non-China capacity expansion.
- CPO is still early; copper remains dominant for in-rack connections for now. Scale-up depends on both technology readiness and economics.

**Implications**:
- Tailwind for non-China InP substrate suppliers and compound semiconductor / optical players (AXT, Largan, VPEC, etc.).
- Potential delays or cost premiums in optical networking buildout if diversification lags.
- Direct relevance to user's photonics cohort (CPO is a key enabling technology for next-gen AI networking and scale-out).
- Watch for CSP capex commentary on optical spend and any further policy moves on InP.

## 4. Passives — High-Spec MLCCs, Chip Resistors & Power Delivery Components

**Core Dynamic**: High-power AI accelerator boards require dramatically more high-capacitance, high-temperature, small-form-factor MLCCs for decoupling and power integrity, plus resistors for various functions. Supply of the most difficult specifications is struggling to keep pace.

**Key Data Points from Recent Coverage**:
- TrendForce: Structural shortage risk for premium MLCC specs in 2H26 as CSP in-house ASIC platforms ramp (Google TPU V8/t/i, AWS Trainium4, Meta MTIA 400/450, AMD MI450, Nvidia Rubin).
  - Example: AMD MI450 platform replaced aluminum electrolytic and tantalum capacitors with MLCCs → 47μF 2.5V X6S 0402 usage jumped from 1,440 to 10,544 units per board.
  - Nvidia Rubin 100μF 4V X6S 0805 usage also increased substantially.
- Suppliers (Murata started mass production of high-spec parts end-2025; Samsung Electro-Mechanics from March 2026; Taiyo Yuden and Kyocera also expanding) but significant yield challenges remain. Murata's new Izumo plant only reaches full operation in 2027.
- Book-to-bill ratios rising since April 2026; lead times for some high-capacity X6S products extended from ~8 weeks to as much as 20 weeks.
- Major CSPs with LTAs expected to receive priority allocation; ODMs/system makers without agreements face higher spot prices and potential shipment delays.
- Chip resistor price increases (Taiwan and China makers: Yageo, Ralec, Fenghua, Walsin, Kamet, Viking) driven by silver price surge (~3x YoY) feeding into silver paste costs + strong AI, industrial control, energy storage, and new energy demand. Yageo noted as being on Nvidia's BOM (resistors start at ~2x standard spec pricing). Japanese makers (Murata, Nippon Chemicon) strategically increasing share of commodity products even at thinner margins to defend against Chinese competition and protect long-term positioning.

**Nuances & Edge Cases**:
- Demand concentration into a narrow set of difficult-to-manufacture specs creates structural tightness even as overall MLCC capacity may appear adequate.
- Silver price volatility is a direct commodity input risk for resistors.
- LTAs create a two-tier market: secured supply for hyperscalers vs. exposure for everyone else.
- Chinese passives makers are both competitors (low-price pressure) and part of the global supply response.

**Implications**:
- Pricing power and volume growth for qualified high-spec suppliers (Yageo, Murata group, Samsung Electro-Mechanics, Taiyo Yuden, etc.).
- Contributes to overall AI system cost inflation but is necessary for reliable high-performance operation.
- Watch for inventory builds by ODMs in 3Q, spot premiums, and any further price hike announcements.
- Overlaps with power delivery and thermal themes (better passives + improved substrates = better PI and system efficiency).

## 5. Custom ASICs, CPUs & Broader AI Silicon Ecosystem

**Core Dynamic**: Hyperscalers and AI companies are increasingly developing or commissioning custom accelerators (in-house ASICs) alongside or instead of general-purpose GPUs. This broadens silicon demand and drives secondary effects in packaging and passives.

**Key Data Points from Recent Coverage**:
- JPMorgan note on AI driving a resurgence in custom chips (ASICs).
- Bernstein Global Semis note on "CPU Renaissance" and a large TAM opportunity.
- Explicit linkage between CSP in-house ASIC ramps and surging demand for high-spec MLCCs (see passives section).
- Broadcom (AVGO) coverage with JPM bullish tone and notes on TPU-related work.
- Intel 18A process yield commentary (~50% at time of note; Apple as primary customer with signed contract).
- NVIDIA N1X (Windows PC AI processor) discussion with skepticism on aggressive shipment volume assumptions relative to MacBook Pro benchmarks.
- Agentic AI cited as a meaningful tailwind for general-purpose CPUs.

**Nuances & Edge Cases**:
- On-device AI (N1X-class) is currently more niche/power-user oriented; broad upgrade cycle depends heavily on OS and application ecosystem support, not just silicon.
- Custom ASIC wins are concentrated; foundry competition (TSMC vs. Intel vs. Samsung) is intensifying.
- Volume realization risk on newer PC AI silicon plays.

**Implications**:
- Broadens the opportunity set beyond pure GPU plays (custom ASIC designers, foundries, and supporting ecosystem).
- Directly fuels packaging and passives demand (larger/more complex boards, higher MLCC counts).
- Watch for design-win announcements, foundry yield progress (especially Intel 18A), and hyperscaler capex commentary on custom silicon mix.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

- **Power Delivery & PI**: Glass substrates and better passives directly improve power integrity (lower resistance/inductance), freeing power headroom for more compute. HVDC ramp mentions (800VDC/660kW context) point to higher-voltage power distribution trends in AI infrastructure.
- **Geopolitics, Talent & Supply Security**: China export controls (InP), talent migration to Chinese universities, and CSP diversification pushes are treated as structural features of the landscape, not temporary noise. Supply security often trumps pure cost.
- **Structural Demand vs. Traditional Cycles**: Recurring argument (especially on Substack) that AI has changed the memory and related semi cycles. Focus shifts from classic boom-bust to who can deliver sustained capacity and technology at the right nodes.
