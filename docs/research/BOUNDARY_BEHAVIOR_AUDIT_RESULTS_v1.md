spot) than the GEX-approach filter (±0.3% of spot). Random controls
were therefore systematically further from spot than GEX levels,
which trivially advantaged them on the bounce metric (further-away
levels need less directional move to qualify as "bounced past +0.3%
without first breaching by 0.2%"). The 40pp bounce-rate gap is a
mechanical artifact of this asymmetry, not a real signal about GEX
boundaries.

The v2 amendment uses distance-matched controls: random level is
sampled at the SAME |spot − level| distance as the GEX level.
