#### **INTRO**

---

It’s not just this period. This absolutely historic and remarkable period in the market. But all of them that I have experienced. All the lessons, hardships, and successes. Combined this with all the knowledge that is learned from those that have gone before and experienced the same ups and downs. That has brough us here and taught us the principles of trading for a lifetime. And they can be broken down into three simple truths.   
  
-Respect the price action of the market  
-Ride your winners  
-Cut your losses  
  
*Aggregated by George Coyle, Author of the Market Wizards book set to come out in June of this year.*   
  
Within them are massive intricacies, adaptations, and self discoveries only forged by living through the hell fire of the market for a few dozen or more cycles. I can teach you my lessons with the hopes that you will learn easier or softer. I can show my entries and ideas with the hopes that you will execute effectively as I know I will. But only you can develop the scar tissue necessary to make it through the trials and tribulations that accompany a trader’s journey.

---

Today, I would like to share a few of those. Some of which might be unpopular, or completely unrelatable for many of you. For some, it might be just what you need to hear.   
  
Cut your losses. Are we talking LOD, previous day’s low, 8% stop, 2% stop? Well, yes. All of that works, if you let it work. If you stick to it. If you allow it to. Chances are, most of your challenges come from meddling, or involving yourself when really you can let your stop be the stop and if it stops you out then you sell. So whether it’s LOD or an arbitrary 3% stop, yes.   
  
Now, many probably are thinking what about moving my stop once a trade starts to work. Yeah there is some nuance. But is there? What if you make it so there isn’t. What if you said when I get up 2R, or up 10%, or up 20% then I can move my stop to breakeven. What if there was something in place instead of “oh I didn’t have cushion to take on “overnight risk”. Which while we here, if anyone says overnight risk as a phrase block them and show them this chart.   
  
*Insert SPX overnight performance chart.*

[![](images/img_002.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V6vs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc03388c8-8776-4883-b4be-843879d79c3a_471x283.png)

Okay back on topic. Point of it is defining where is my stop. And letting it be! I enter on the daily chart or the hourly chart. What good will a five minute upper wick do for me? Wouldn’t I be better off placing a hard stop order in my broker and then go over to my journal and playbook the trade process and plan? I’ll bet my ass and a half that almost all of us would be better off doing that. Even something like I keep my stop my stop until the 20EMA comes above my entry, then I will move my stop to breakeven and trail the 20EMA close. Then when the 50SMA crosses above my entry I will trail the 50SMA. Of course, you have to balance that out with the math of withstanding a drawdown to that spot. And in that case you should trim to be able to trail that planned stop.   
  
Anyways, point of it is, have a criteria. Write it down. And do it. I want to read this quote from Christian Flanders that he posted on Friday.

[![](images/img_003.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pR55!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdcb0540d-032d-4af9-8e4d-857bed5d0fec_596x151.png)

I decided I’d rather take the pain of following my rules than the pain of not following them. What a perfect phrase to motivate you to accept the uncertain outcome of the trade.

---

Ride your winners. Okay yeah, but how. Sure it would be great to buy SNDK at $60 over IPO highs and trail a 50SMA all the way to $1500. Yes, it would! But could you? Do your thoughts and actions even allow for that to occur? Do you have guardrails in place to allow for these outliers. Or are you trimming your NVDA at $540 on Jan 9th, 2024 to lock some in. Not all will be SNDK, NVDA, or anything of the sort. Most will be forgettable small wins. Or small losses. But you should have something in place to allow for outliers to greatly benefit your account. And of course, accept the circumstances that come with that. Such as drawdown to the 9ema, 20ema, 50sma, previous support level.   
  
For me, I like to simply do the math. If I were to trail all my positions with a 20EMA stop would the drawdown be too much to handle. Does the circumstances of the market backdrop encourage me to allow for that drawdown to potentially occur. Meaning, are we in a historically hot fucking period where semis are literally doubling and tripling in days and weeks?!?!? YES lol what. So why wouldn’t I accept a measly 9EMA trail on MU while I am up 75%. Why wouldn’t I accept a drawdown on ARM when I am up 95% and it can barely stay red for a session. Sure I see the extension, so does everyone. But this is obviously special and every period won’t be. If the math doesn’t fit within that defined framework then I will sell a portion to allow for me to mange the trade within that framework, again, accepting the uncertain outcome just as I do with my stops. Knowing where but not when I will be exiting, and not knowing what will happen in between.

---

Respect the price action of the market. What a powerful statement. Takes a bit of ego to not do so right? Everyone is wrong but me. I know something everyone does not. I see something that most are oblivious to.   
  
Or, respect the price action of the market. Could be trade with the trend for trend following traders, historically the most successful and broad group of market participants. Either way it means allow the market to validate or invalidate and you are just there to execute within that structure of the multi Trillion Dollar marketplace we have here in the US.   
  
It could also mean truly internalizing that the market is a discounting mechanism and often prices in forward looking events or circumstances before it reaches the masses. This speaks to something I find to be critical as a trader. TRUST. Trust the market, trust the price action. Have a healthy relationship with the market and your “read”. Those that look on with skepticism, doubt, and disbelief are often mirroring their own true internals onto the market. Those than can trust and believe with confidence in themselves and the market will undoubtedly see more success.

---

In conclusion, I encourage you to do a few things after this cycle concludes and when the market goes into hard mode again. Write down your goals, your principles, your objectives. Write down your strategy. Write down your rules within that strategy. Write down what encourages participation and what means it’s simply time to watch. Give yourself a framework to navigate within otherwise you will continue to get in the way of the potential that the market has to offer.   
  
Let’s have a great week folks!

## **Bracco’s Breakdown & Top Ideas**

---

Another absolutely insane week of price action, specifically in the QQQ and Semiconductors. I hate to be this guy and ruin the party, but some of these names are beginning to fill out my criteria for a parabolic short. Nobody really likes when I write about these in Sunday Scans, but have been pretty accurate so far this year with my write-ups on SLV GLD in January and KOSPI KORU in February. They are very tough trades and don’t need to be followed, but I also love to help someone riding a big unrealized long position take advantage and trim a bit into a parabolic extension.   
  
I have not traded any of the semiconductors to the short side yet on this recent rally as none of them really set-up, just pure strength. But as of Friday’s close a few names are starting to check the boxes, opening the window of opportunity for this week.   
  
Let’s first start with the QQQ. We are really starting to accelerate to the upside here, closing completely outside of the upper Bollinger Band after Friday’s 2.3% gain. The QQQ is now 9.91 ATR multiples above the 50-day moving average, its largest extension since inception in March of 1999. I am not looking for some massive top here, but it is hard to imagine that this does not result in a sharp pullback off the highs. Then again, I have seen more than a few historic moves this year that I would have never expected. Point is, if a few names set up for parabolic shorts, the QQQ extension helps support that thesis.

[![](images/img_030.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YyCA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F83896cdc-be04-469a-8b31-68a41e600b07_2556x1265.png)

---

The four names I will be watching closely this week are INTC SNDK MU. Below I have attached a spreadsheet of parabolic extensions I have tracked over the years. I studied their ATR multiples from 50MA the day before the peak as well as the % extension above specific moving averages. ATR multiples above the 50MA from Jeff Sun beat simple percent gains because they adjust for the stock’s unique volatility. That is what I like to focus on the most. Helps reveal true over-extensions in parabolic moves that just straight up percentages often mislead imo.  
  
Below the averages I attached the current statistics for the four names mentioned above. The green highlighted boxes = above the average in which all the names above peaked at. One characteristic that is extremely important when identifying a parabolic advance is consecutive weeks of range expansion. We recently saw this with Silver in January when it put together 3 consecutive weeks of range expansion before it’s climax, as well as many more classic, model book examples throughout the years.

Consecutive weeks of range expansion signal exponential price progression, a hallmark of parabolic curves. It is a week by week build up into a climactic reversal. The cherry on top is a signifigant range expansion week on the 3rd or 4th week of the burst, which is typically the final week of the move.
