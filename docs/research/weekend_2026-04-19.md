## Executive Summary

TSMC's Q1 print and Q2 guide reset the bar for AI-driven semi earnings, with record profits and $52–56B capex confirming that leading-edge (N3/N2) and advanced packaging remain the sector's choke points. Meanwhile, Tesla/Intel's "TeraFab" initiative and Tesla's AI5 tape-out at Samsung SF2T introduce the first credible multi-year challenge to TSMC's foundry dominance, even as NVIDIA reportedly reaffirms its next-gen LPU at TSMC. ## Key Themes

### 1. **TSMC Delivers Blowout Q1, Raises Capex to Top of Range**
TSMC reported 1Q26 net income of NT$572.5B (+58% YoY), EPS NT$22.08, and gross margin of 66.2%, beating expectations. Q2 guidance calls for revenue of $39.0–40.2B (+~32% YoY, +10% QoQ), gross margin of 65.5–67.5%, and full-year capex at the top end of the $52–56B range. N3 capacity is tightening on AI demand, with Arizona Fab 2 N3 volume production pushed to 2H27 and Kumamoto to 2028.
*Sources: TrendForce (Apr 16), Global Semi Research (Apr 13)*

### 2. **TeraFab and Samsung's Tesla Win Open a Second Foundry Front**
Tesla's AI5 chip completed tape-out on Samsung's SF2T domestic line ("KR2613" marking), using SK hynix memory and Samsung LPDDR5X, with AI6 to follow. Separately, Tesla and Intel's "TeraFab" initiative has drawn industry attention, with Intel set to brief staff on scope in the coming weeks. TSMC Chairman C.C. Wei acknowledged Intel and Samsung as competitors but reportedly confirmed NVIDIA's next-gen LPU project stays at TSMC—"no shortcuts in foundry."
*Sources: TrendForce (Apr 15–16)*

### 3. **Advanced Packaging and 2nm Backlog Builds Through 2027**
The Meta–Broadcom AI chip alliance is reportedly targeting TSMC 2nm with CoWoS-L packaging for a 1H27 launch, adding to an already tight advanced-node/packaging pipeline. This reinforces the view that leading-edge capacity, not wafer starts broadly, is the binding constraint on AI silicon supply into 2027.
*Sources: TrendForce (Apr 16), Global Semi Research (Apr 13)*

### 4. **Optical Interconnect Becomes a First-Order AI Constraint**
The Diligence Stack frames connectivity as expanding from $25–35B today toward $67–96B by decade's end, with TSMC's COUPE silicon photonics platform positioned to replicate the CoWoS playbook. AXT is flagged as a key indium phosphide substrate play levered to ~85% industry growth in optical interconnect, while Marvell is highlighted as a structural AI infrastructure winner via UALink, CXL, Google LPU, and memory architecture exposure. San'an Optoelectronics also announced advances in high-speed optical chips.
*Sources: Global Semi Research (Apr 16, Apr 18), Diligence Stack (Mar 26), TrendForce (Apr 16)*

### 5. **Memory, Power, and Cooling Frame the Next Bottlenecks**
Global Semi Research argues memory capex is structurally under-invested—not cyclical excess—and that long-term agreements won't bind suppliers in tight markets. The Diligence Stack highlights 800 VDC power redesign for 120–300+ kW AI racks, liquid cooling as a thermal prerequisite for scale, and a memory/storage layer collapse. Neoclouds are described as gaining ground on hyperscalers through 2027 due to power-availability arbitrage.
*Sources: Global Semi Research (Apr 9, Apr 7), Diligence Stack (Mar 31, Apr 9, Apr 14, Mar 21)*

- **Samsung winning Tesla AI5/AI6** signals leading-edge customer diversification away from TSMC earlier than expected.
