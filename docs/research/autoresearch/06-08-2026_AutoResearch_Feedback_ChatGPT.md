## Why the autonomous loop is mostly a trap

The first failure mode is **public-idea crowding**. Finance has repeatedly shown that once a signal becomes known, its returns fall. McLean and Pontiff study 97 published return predictors and find that returns drop **26% out of sample** and **58% after publication**, implying a meaningful publication-induced decay on top of data-mining effects. That matters directly here: a scraper that reads public papers, social posts, and teardowns is, by construction, fishing in the same pond everyone else can see. ### The attack on continuous multiple testing

The multiple-testing body count is severe. The next gate should be a **cheap rejection layer**, not a cheap acceptance layer.
