# Internal-Validity Backtest ‚Äî Week of 2026-04-13

**Data horizon**: one week. `soe_signals`, `signal_outcomes`,
and `broker_roundtrips` all start 2026-04-13. No earlier history
exists in `snapshots.db`. Treat this as stability-within-week,
not a multi-week test.

## 1. Raw SOE engine directional edge

For each signal, the engine specified a direction (BULL/BEAR).
At trigger_ts + 1d we check: did spot move in that direction at
all (`any-hit`)? By at least 50bp (`50bp-hit`)?

This measures the **engine's raw quality**, independent of the
user's contract selection, hold duration, or options math.

| Grade | Dir | N | Avg 1d return | Any-hit | 50bp-hit |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| A | BULL | 67 | 0.59% | 38.8% | 34.3% |
| A | BEAR | 2 | 1.30% | 0.0% | 0.0% |
| B | BULL | 83 | 0.78% | 66.3% | 54.2% |
| B | BEAR | 2 | 1.95% | 0.0% | 0.0% |
| B+ | BULL | 919 | 1.17% | 61.8% | 52.7% |
| B+ | BEAR | 3 | 3.96% | 33.3% | 33.3% |
| C | BULL | 240 | 2.81% | 94.6% | 85.4% |
| C | BEAR | 4 | 1.95% | 0.0% | 0.0% |
| SCALP | BULL | 6 | 0.84% | 100.0% | 50.0% |
| SCALP | BEAR | 3 | 0.40% | 0.0% | 0.0% |

**Read:**
- A BULL at 38.8% any-hit is a flag ‚Äî this week's A-grade engine
  was directionally worse than coin-flip. User's 100% WR on the
  8 A-grade broker trades is survivorship: user took A signals
  with additional confluence.
- B+ BULL at 61.8% any-hit on n=919 is real edge ‚Äî 11.8 pp above
  coin-flip across a large sample.
- BEAR samples are tiny (<10 each) ‚Äî can't evaluate.

## 2. Bootstrap CI on 72.5% broker WR

10,000 resamples of the 91 roundtrips with replacement.
90% CI tells us the range of WR we'd plausibly see if this
week's market were replayed many times.

- **Point estimate WR**: 72.5%
- **90% CI on WR**: [64.8%, 80.2%]
- **Point estimate net P&L**: $+11,569
- **90% CI on net P&L**: [$+6,932, $+16,380]

**Read:**
- A 90% CI of [64.8%, 80.2%]
  means even a bad draw from this week's trade distribution
  stays above 64.8%. The 72.5% headline isn't a
  100-to-1 fluke ‚Äî BUT the CI is generated by resampling the
  same 91 trades, so it captures within-sample variance, not
  between-week variance. **You could still see 50% WR next week**
  if market regime shifts. This is a floor check, not a prophecy.

## 3. Rule validation on all 1,329 SOE signals

The broker-trade simulation earlier tested rules on 91 trades.
Here we test them on the full **engine output** (A/A+/B+ signals
regardless of whether you took them). This is the population-level
answer to 'does the rule improve engine quality or just selection?'

Hit metric = 50bp-directional at 1d (signal-level, not P&L).

| Rule | Signals kept | Hit rate | Avg 1d return | Œî Hit rate |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Baseline | 991 | 51.3% | 1.14% | ‚Äî |
| Rule #1 (block BEAR single-name) | 986 | 51.4% | 1.13% | +0.2pp |
| Rule #2 (DTE >= 3) | 963 | 51.3% | 1.15% | +0.0pp |
| #1 + #2 combined | 958 | 51.5% | 1.15% | +0.2pp |

**Read:**
- If Rule #1 or #2 *keeps* hit rate while cutting signal volume,
  they're pure noise-filters ‚Äî good.
- If they *drop* hit rate significantly, they're cutting real
  winners alongside noise ‚Äî reconsider.

## 4. What this is NOT

- **Not a multi-week backtest.** All data is one week.
- **Not regime-stable.** This week SPY was up roughly every day.
  A bull-tape week inflates call hit rates and punishes puts.
- **Not out-of-sample.** The same week's data was used to *derive*
  the rules (cohort analysis) and to *test* them. Signal-level
  validation on all 1,329 helps a little (signals not in the 91)
  but remains the same calendar window.

### Paths to real multi-week validation

1. **Export prior broker CSVs.** Weeks of 2026-04-06, 2026-03-30,
   2026-03-23 would double or triple sample size. Drop into
   `data/trades/` and run:
   ```

   python -m scripts.attribute_trades_to_signals
   python -m scripts.analyze_week_cohorts
   python -m scripts.simulate_rule_changes
   ```
   Assumes SOE engine and signal_outcomes don't have history ‚Äî
   attribution will be NONE for most prior trades. But cohort
   stats (DTE/direction/hold) still work.

2. **ThetaData SOE replay.** With `thetadata.py` live we can
   reconstruct historical GEX state and re-run `generate_signals()`
   against past days. Yields full multi-week SOE history but
   is a multi-session project.

3. **Forward walk.** Just let the system run. By end of May we'll
   have 6+ weeks of native signal_outcomes and broker trades
   without any replay effort.
