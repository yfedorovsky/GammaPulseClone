## 2. Setup Context — What the Chart Looked Like Before April 10

MSFT entered April 2026 in a multi-week pullback after failing to hold highs near $400 in mid-March. The stock had been grinding lower in a controlled, low-volatility descent — classic institutional distribution disguised as a gentle fade.

**Pre-breakout conditions (late March – April 10):**

- **Price structure**: Orderly pullback from ~$400 to the $370 zone. No panic selling, no capitulation wicks — a measured retracement that built a base.
- **Moving averages**: Price sitting below both SMA10 (~$372) and SMA20 (~$376–$377), creating a compressed coil. SMA50 still rising underneath, confirming the longer-term uptrend was intact.
- **Volume**: Declining throughout the pullback — textbook. Sellers were losing conviction, not gaining it. The 20-day average volume was ~33M shares; the pullback days were printing 25–30M.
- **RSI / Momentum**: Washed out into the 40–45 zone. Not oversold enough to trigger mean-reversion algos, but low enough to give the next move room to run without hitting overbought immediately.
- **Volatility compression**: Bollinger Bands narrowing, ATR contracting. The coiled spring was loading.
- **Sector context**: Broader SaaS/software had been weak YTD. MSFT was not the only name sitting on a pullback shelf — the entire sector was positioned for a rotation bid.

**Key read**: The setup was a low-volatility base below declining short-term MAs with volume drying up — the exact precondition for a snap-back breakout. Smart money was done selling; the tape was waiting for a match.

## 3. Technical Breakdown

### 3.1 Pattern Recognition

The structure resolves as a **V-shaped reversal with cup-handle characteristics**. The pullback from mid-March carved the left side of the cup. The April 10 session ($370.87 close) marked the handle low — a final shakeout on below-average volume before the reversal fired.

What made this pattern high-conviction:
- **Handle depth was shallow** (<3% below the cup floor), indicating buyers were impatient and stepping in early.
- **No lower low** relative to the March pullback trough — higher-low formation confirmed.
- **Breakout candle (April 13)** cleared the handle resistance and the cup rim in a single session.

### 3.2 Moving Average Dynamics

| MA | Level (April 10) | Reclaimed On | Held As Support? |
|---|---|---|---|
| SMA10 | ~$372 | April 13 | Yes — never retested |
| SMA20 | ~$376–$377 | April 13 | Yes — gapped above on Day 2 |
| SMA50 | ~$365 (est.) | Never lost | Rising floor throughout |

**Critical nuance**: The SMA10/SMA20 crossover was negative heading into April 13 (SMA10 below SMA20 — short-term bearish). The breakout candle blew through both MAs in a single session and flipped the cross back to bullish by close. This "cross-reclaim" pattern is an extremely high-signal momentum trigger — it traps shorts who were leaning on the bearish cross and forces aggressive covering.

### 3.3 Volume Signature

| Date | Volume | vs. 20-Day Avg | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| April 10 | 28.1M | 0.9x | Quiet setup — supply exhaustion |
| April 13 | 35.7M | 1.1x | Above-average breakout — institutional entry |
| April 14 | 37.5M | 1.1x | Sustained — no one-day wonder |
| April 15 | 45.0M | **1.3x** | Spike — late momentum + short covering |

**The volume tells the story**: This was not a single-day spike followed by fade. Volume *expanded* across all three sessions — the hallmark of genuine institutional accumulation, not retail FOMO. Day 1 was the trigger, Day 2 was confirmation, Day 3 was the capitulation chase.

**Volume climax read**: The 1.3x spike on Day 3 suggests the move was entering its terminal acceleration phase. The smart entry was Day 1 or early Day 2; Day 3 was the gift for those already positioned, not a fresh entry.

### 3.4 Candle Analysis

- **April 10**: Small-body red candle with a lower wick — sellers tried, buyers absorbed. Classic pre-breakout "quiet day."
- **April 13**: Wide-range green candle closing near the high ($384.37 close vs. $384.54 high). Minimal upper wick = no resistance overhead, pure demand.
- **April 14**: Green candle with a small body relative to range — slight hesitation, but held gains and printed a higher high. Gap-up open confirmed overnight conviction.
- **April 15**: Massive range candle ($396.73 low to $414.37 high = $17.64 range), closing in the upper third. This is the "explosion" candle — the type that shows up on scanners and pulls in momentum traders from every timeframe.

## 6. Unusual / Rare Elements

1. **Historic streak rarity**: Three consecutive 2%+ daily gains in MSFT has occurred only 3 times since 2000. This is not noise — it's a regime signal indicating something structural changed in positioning.

2. **Volume expansion across all three days**: Most "rips" show a single-day volume spike followed by declining volume. MSFT showed *rising* volume each day — the signature of institutional accumulation, not retail chasing.

3. **No single catalyst**: There was no earnings beat, no M&A, no FDA approval. The move was driven by *thematic rotation* — a harder catalyst to identify in real-time but more durable when it occurs because there's no "sell the news" event to cap the move.

4. **No whale prints**: Options flow was distributed, not concentrated. No single block trade or unusual sweep drove the action. This suggests broad institutional consensus, not a single fund's conviction bet.

5. **Pre-earnings window**: Q3 earnings were 2 weeks away (April 29). The move happened in the quiet period — meaning insiders weren't buying (blackout window), and the bid was purely external money rotating in.

6. **Sector sympathy context**: YTD SaaS weakness meant MSFT was cheap relative to its own trend. The breakout was partly "catch-up" — the sector had underperformed, and when rotation hit, the bounce was amplified by the depth of the prior drawdown.

## 7. Basis Model Framework — 10-Point Scanner Template

This is the repeatable template for identifying the next explosive multi-day runner. Each point is scored 0–2. **A score of 14+ out of 20 is actionable.**

### Point 1: Multi-Week Pullback Into a Base (0–2)

- **2 pts**: 3–6 week orderly pullback (no panic, controlled selling) with price sitting at or below SMA20.
- **1 pt**: Pullback exists but is choppy or shallow (<5% from recent high).
- **0 pts**: No pullback — stock already extended above MAs.

### Point 2: Volume Drying Up on the Pullback (0–2)

- **2 pts**: Volume declining for 5+ sessions, with the most recent session at <0.9x the 20-day average.
- **1 pt**: Volume mixed but trending lower.
- **0 pts**: Volume steady or increasing during the pullback (distribution, not basing).

### Point 3: Breakout Day Volume Surge (0–2)

- **2 pts**: First green breakout day prints >=1.1x 20-day average volume AND price closes above SMA10.
- **1 pt**: Volume above average but price doesn't clear SMA10 decisively.
- **0 pts**: Breakout on average or below-average volume (unreliable).

### Point 4: Price Reclaims SMA10 + SMA20 (0–2)

- **2 pts**: Price closes above both SMA10 and SMA20 on Day 1 of the move.
- **1 pt**: Closes above SMA10 only; SMA20 still overhead.
- **0 pts**: Fails to reclaim either MA.

### Point 5: Gap-Up Confirmation on Day 2 (0–2)

- **2 pts**: Day 2 opens above Day 1's close (gap-up) and holds above Day 1's high for the full session.
- **1 pt**: Gap-up open but fills the gap intraday (partial conviction).
- **0 pts**: Day 2 opens below Day 1's close (dead cat bounce risk).

> **Mir Cross-Ref**: The gap-up is a valid *signal read* (confirms overnight institutional buying), but the *trade action* is to harvest partial profits at the open, NOT add. Gap-ups create "terrible trade location" for new capital. The add opportunity comes later — on the intraday pullback to VWAP or 8 EMA, if $ADD holds >2K.

### Point 6: Volume Sustains or Expands on Day 2+ (0–2)

- **2 pts**: Volume on Day 2 is >= Day 1 volume (institutional follow-through, not retail fade).
- **1 pt**: Volume declines slightly but remains above the 20-day average.
- **0 pts**: Volume drops below average (momentum dying).

> **Mir Cross-Ref**: Confirmed as a core active filter, not a nice-to-have. Mir actively tracks multi-day volume expansion as the primary confirmation of institutional accumulation. Rising consecutive-day volume is what allows him to hold "runners" with conviction. Without it, the move is suspect.

### Point 7: Identifiable Thematic Catalyst (0–2)

- **2 pts**: Clear sector rotation or thematic catalyst (AI, infrastructure, policy shift) with news flow escalating across multiple days.
- **1 pt**: Single-day catalyst (analyst upgrade, one headline) — may not sustain.
- **0 pts**: No identifiable catalyst (pure technical — lower conviction for multi-day hold).

### Point 8: Wide-Range Candles Closing Near Highs (0–2)

- **2 pts**: Breakout candle(s) have wide intraday range and close within the top 25% of the day's range.
- **1 pt**: Green candle but closes mid-range (some resistance overhead).
- **0 pts**: Doji, spinning top, or long upper wick on the breakout day (rejection).

### Point 9: Rare Streak / Regime Signal (0–2)

- **2 pts**: The move includes a statistical rarity (e.g., consecutive 2%+ days that have occurred <5 times in the stock's modern history).
- **1 pt**: Strong but not historically rare (consecutive green days with expanding volume, but gains <2% each).
- **0 pts**: Ordinary price action.

### Point 10: Options Flow Alignment (0–2)

- **2 pts**: Call volume outpacing puts, no single whale print — distributed institutional flow. Low pre-move IV suggesting the move was not "priced in."
- **1 pt**: Mixed signals — some call buying but elevated put activity or high IV.
- **0 pts**: Heavy put buying, elevated IV, or options market pricing a fade.

## 9. Risks & Lessons

### What Could Have Gone Wrong

1. **Macro reversal**: A hawkish Fed statement, tariff escalation, or geopolitical shock on any of the three days would have killed momentum. The move relied on a calm macro backdrop.

2. **Earnings overhang fade**: With Q3 due April 29, there was risk of "sell the rip into earnings" — institutional desks booking gains ahead of the report. This didn't materialize because the catalyst was thematic (AI capex), not company-specific.

3. **Gap-fill risk on Day 2**: The gap-up open on April 14 ($387.92 vs. prior close $384.37) created a gap that could have filled and trapped breakout buyers. It held — confirmation of strength. **Lesson**: Set a stop at the gap-fill level ($384.37) on Day 2 entries. If the gap fills, the thesis weakens.

4. **Sector correlation drag**: If the broader SaaS rally had failed or reversed, MSFT's move could have stalled on Day 2 or 3. Monitor sector ETFs (XLK, IGV) for confirmation that the rotation is real.

5. **Volume exhaustion signal on Day 3**: The 1.3x volume spike on Day 3 is both a strength and a warning. Parabolic acceleration with volume spikes often marks the *end* of a move, not the beginning. **Lesson**: Day 3 of an explosion is for scaling out, not scaling in.

### Why It Didn't Go Wrong

- **Volume quality**: Each day's volume was higher than the last — not a blow-off spike.
- **Catalyst durability**: AI infrastructure is a multi-quarter theme, not a single headline. No "sell the news" event.
- **Technical structure**: The pullback was orderly, the base was clean, and the breakout was decisive. No overhead supply from prior distribution.
- **Market regime**: Broad risk-on tilt with sector rotation favoring tech. The tide was rising.
