1. It's a leveraged momentum story.
The core is Mir-style relative strength / leadership + sector concentration selecting short-term winners, then monetized with 7–14 DTE calls that give you gamma convexity on 2–3 day bursts. The +140% curated / +138% PIT P&L is almost entirely that: momentum persistence (well-documented factor) + options leverage amplifying the fat tail of the winners while the -50% stop and partials try to cap the losers. GEX, RTS, breadth, trend-day mode, and the 15-minute EMA pullback are secondary filters layered on top. They don't create the edge; they compress it. Strip the options leverage and you get the "small stock avg return" you already measured. This is not mysterious alpha—it's trend-following with extra juice and a lot of scaffolding. It's tradable in the right regime, but calling it a "real edge" independent of momentum is self-deception.2. PIT quarterly resolves most of the obvious survivorship/hindsight on the universe, but not everything.
Freezing sectors quarterly on a composite score and then scanning inside them is legitimately strong anti-bias work. Capturing 99% of the curated edge while random sectors produce garbage is evidence the concentration adds value and isn't pure look-ahead. But hidden contamination remains: your composite sector score itself—if it has any optimized weights, thresholds, or look-back windows that were tuned across the same 15 months—still embeds hindsight. Quarterly is better than daily, but with only ~5–6 rotation cycles in 15 months you have almost no statistical power to test whether the selector itself survives regime shifts. It's cleaner than curated, but not bulletproof.3. The most likely remaining illusions are exactly the ones options backtests always hide.  Option repricing / liquidity bucket distortion: Your backtest almost certainly uses mid prices or synthetic fills on "sector leaders." Real single-stock option spreads (even liquid names) are 5–15% round-trip on short holds when you factor in the bid-ask walk and adverse selection. 0DTE stale-spot blocks and SPY/QQQ scalp logic don't fix the 7–14 DTE names.  
Moneyness and path dependency: You mention MFE analysis was done, but if you're assuming fixed delta or ignoring how gamma decays and vega reacts to IV crush on non-event days, the +100% targets are fantasy. Fast resolution helps, but you're still riding realized gamma that disappears the moment the burst stalls.  
Hidden concentration and capital recycling: 34 tickers but sector-concentrated = correlated blow-ups when one theme breaks. Fast 2–3 day holds look great on paper for recycling, but options margin, settlement, and the fact that you can't always exit the partial at +25% without slippage in live markets kills the compounding.  
Operational/live slippage: Mac Mini + Discord bridge + paper portfolio is not execution realism. Latency, signal parsing errors, partial-fill reality, and the psychological difference between paper and real P&L (especially on -50% stops) are massive gaps. Trend-day mode was added reactively after a live under-participation session—that's the definition of post-hoc rule fitting.
The tail-driven nature you already flag is the biggest red flag: most of the P&L is probably 5–10 monster winners. One bad regime and it evaporates.

4. GEX is not doing the heavy lifting.
Mir / momentum is doing everything important for selection and timing. GEX is a quality gate and structure map—useful for context (floors, ceilings, regime flavor), but secondary at best. Positive GEX regimes correlate heavily with the bullish momentum environments you're already trading; it's not independent alpha. Blunt truth: if your A/B framework (which you already have running) shows Mir+GEX only marginally better than Mir-only after 3–6 months of live paper (statistically significant, not noisy), then GEX is just expensive confirmation bias. The outcome that would actually convince me GEX is useful: Mir+GEX delivers materially higher Sharpe, lower max drawdown, and better Calmar on walk-forward or extended paper with zero further parameter tweaks. Anything less and it's noise.5. The discipline layer is a real moat, but the edge is still mostly signal + instrument + exits.

It is not overstated—most people with similar signals fail here. But let's allocate honestly: ~60–70% of the observed P&L is from the momentum/sector signal + options leverage on fast bursts. ~15–20% from the exit ladder and partials. ~10–15% from GEX/regime filters.
