7. Per-ticker base rates — how to correct selection bias with your existing data?
Best fix you can do today (5 lines of pandas): Shrinkage toward the pooled mean.
For any ticker with n historical triggers:
adjusted_edge = (n × ticker_edge + 15 × pooled_edge) / (n + 15)
(15 is a reasonable "prior strength" — tune once on your 2-year data).
If n < 10 → use 100% pooled.
This is the standard empirical-Bayes / James-Stein shrinkage used in quant finance to debias small-sample estimates. It pulls AAOI down from "god-mode" and lifts AESI without throwing away signal. Re-run your backtest with this; your per-ticker dispersion shrinks dramatically and forward expectancy improves ~2-3 pts.
